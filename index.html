Building a Technological Risk Framework for Energy Companies

Engineering First

Over the past weeks, I've been developing a structured, engineering-focused framework to assess technological risk in energy companies.

This isn't an ESG checklist. It's not a generic cyber score. It's a full operational risk model grounded in how real energy infrastructure actually works.

What the framework evaluates

The model assesses technological exposure across six core domains:

01

OT Infrastructure & Reliability

02

Cyber-Physical (IT/OT) Risk

03

Obsolescence & Vendor Risk

04

System Complexity & Integration

05

Monitoring & Predictive Capability

06

Governance & Organizational Risk

Each domain is scored using structured engineering criteria, normalized to a 0–100 index, and weighted to reflect today's industry realities in the U.S. energy sector.

The result: a transparent, auditable Technological Risk Index designed for engineers first.
